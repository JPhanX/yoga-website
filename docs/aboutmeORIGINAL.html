About Me

I’ve been practicing yoga for over 10 years, since 2014. Over time, this practice has supported me both physically and mentally, and it’s something I’m passionate about sharing with others.

My approach has evolved from striving for “perfect” poses to a slower, more intuitive practice that prioritises strength, balance, and restoration. I also encourage choosing the variation that best supports your body on any given day — there is no single “advanced” version, only what feels right for you.

My teaching offers a calm, supportive space where you can move with intention, build strength gently, and create space to rest.

My Journey

My practice has been shaped over many years through a blend of movement, strength, and stillness. I am experienced in Pilates, Ashtanga, and Vinyasa yoga, and these influences naturally appear in my teaching.

During my corporate career, yoga and Pilates became a grounding part of my routine — helping me manage stress, reset during the day, and support both physical and mental wellbeing.

Over time, my practice shifted from dynamic, energetic movement toward a more balanced approach — combining strength with slower, restorative styles of yoga and rest practices.

Teaching Style

My teaching focuses on slow, intentional movement that builds strength without intensity. Classes are grounded in breath-led Vinyasa, with space to pause, reset, and move with awareness.

I prioritise stability, mobility, and nervous system support, creating a practice that feels steady rather than fast-paced or performance-driven.

Each class is designed to be accessible, whether you’re building consistency in your practice or looking to balance strength with rest and recovery.

I encourage you to listen to your body and move at your own pace. I always offer options throughout class, recognising that every body is different and each practice will feel unique.

Teaching Experience

I have taught Vinyasa and restorative-based classes in studio settings, including covering classes in Leeds.

My teaching is shaped by both personal practice and student feedback, with a focus on creating a calm, supportive environment that encourages steady progress rather than intensity or performance-based movement.

I continue to develop my teaching through ongoing practice, study, and group teaching experience.

I find real joy in seeing students develop confidence, strength, and ease in their practice over time.
